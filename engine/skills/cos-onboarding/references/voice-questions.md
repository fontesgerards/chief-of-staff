# Voice question bank (cos-onboarding Step 4)

> Pulled by the onboarding runbook. Voice is the thing the principal notices first when it's wrong, so this step goes deep. **Observe first** (pull 3–5 real sent messages across contexts), draft, then fill gaps with these — one at a time.

**Always start by gathering samples, not asking:**
> "Paste (or point me at) 3–5 messages you've actually sent recently — mix of contexts: a quick one, a careful one, an external one. I'll derive your voice from these rather than guessing."

Then derive a draft and confirm-by-exception. Fill remaining gaps:

1. How would a close colleague describe your writing in one line?
2. How do you sign off — by context (internal vs external, quick vs formal)?
3. Quirks: emoji? exclamation points? em-dashes? lowercase? abbreviations?
4. **What is your voice NOT?** (e.g. "not corporate, not hedgy, never 'I hope this finds you well'")
5. **Words/phrases you'd never use** — the banned list. (AI tells: "delve," "leverage," "it's worth noting," "in today's fast-paced…")
6. Where does your tone shift — and how? (casual with the team, precise with the board, warm with customers)

## Output shape

Write the **constraints** to `core/voice.md` (within its `budget_chars`):
- **Lead with** · **Length defaults** · **Avoid** · **Register** · **What it's NOT** · **Banned (short list)**

Overflow to `procedural/drafting.md` (unbounded):
- The full banned list, **annotated real samples** (the message + why it's "you"), and per-context register notes.

> Voice touches `core/` → **gate it**: show the drafted voice and get an explicit yes before writing. `#voice` corrections later refine it (Tier-2).
